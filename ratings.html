Photomode Rankings

  1. Stray
    • Stray doesn't have a photomode. With some clever camera angles, UI adjustments, and creativity you can certainly photograph this game. Somebody had to be last place and without a photomode it had to be Stray.
  2. Control
    • Control has a very simple photomode. It offers some unique filters and little else. The outstanding environmental design does a lot of heavy lifting when it comes to great virtual photography of the game. While i personally enjoy the challenge of a simple photomode, it's not for everyone.
  3. The Witcher 3
    • The Witcher 3's photomode is a newer addition introduced with the Playstation 5 upgrade. It's a relatvely standard photomode. There are no options for posing, facial expressions, or environmental changes. The highlight of this photomode is the excellent camera distance, only beat by Ghost of Tsushima.
  4. Death Stranding
    • Death Stranding has a great photomode. There are many options for character poses and facial expressions. The photomode offers more options for portrait style photos than landscapes.
  5. Horizon: Forbidden West
    • Forbidden West's photomode is very similar to Horizon: Zero Dawn's photomode. Luckily Horizon: Zero Dawn had a solid photomode! HFW has an excellent selection of character poses and facial expressions. There is a vast selection of face paint for Aloy. Landscape shots were considered as well with the ability to change time of day and a decent camera distance.
  6. Ghost of Tsushima
    • An easy choice for first place. Ghost of Tsushima's photomode is only limited by your creativity.
